# pamgolet-sales-analysis
It Analyzes and visualize business performance for a company called Pamgolet across two core areas — sales and profits. The presentation spans five slides: a title slide establishing the source and timestamp, a summary slide consolidating key findings, a recommendations slide presenting actionable conclusions drawn from the analysis, a profits slide covering profitability performance including profit by product, segment, and country, and a sales slide examining revenue trends, top-performing products, and units sold across the years 2013 and 2014. Key highlights from the data include Paseo emerging as the best-selling and most profitable product with 338K units sold and $4.80M in profit, the United States leading all countries with $25.03M in total sales, the Government segment generating the highest profit at $11.4M, and the Enterprise segment recording a loss of $614.55K — with the group recommending that Pamgolet leverage Paseo's success, review Enterprise operations, capitalize on Government partnerships, and continue expanding in high-performing regions such as the U.S., Canada, and France.
The Pamgolet Sales and Profit Dashboard is a business intelligence project designed to analyze and visualize the company's sales performance and profitability across products, countries, and customer segments over a two-year period. The dashboard gives management a consolidated view of key metrics — total units sold, sales value, profit distribution, and geographical performance — enabling data-driven decisions around growth, pricing, and market strategy.

# The Challenge
The core challenge was making sense of performance data that varied significantly across multiple dimensions. Sales grew dramatically from 175 units in 2013 to 525 units in 2014 — a 200% increase — yet profitability told a more complex story. While Paseo dominated both sales and profit, the Enterprise segment recorded a loss of $614.55K despite being an established customer group, raising serious questions about pricing inefficiencies and operational costs. Additionally, Mexico showed the lowest sales figures among all countries, yet certain products still generated strong profits there — an underexplored opportunity that raw numbers alone could not explain.

# Technical Solution
The team addressed these challenges by building a multi-page Power BI dashboard covering dedicated views for Sales and Profits. Interactive visualizations were used to break down performance by product, country, customer segment, and time period — moving beyond simple totals to surface the "why" behind the numbers. Key analytical outputs included a profit-by-segment breakdown revealing the Government segment as the strongest contributor at $11.4M, a product-level profitability ranking led by Paseo at $4.80M, country-level sales comparisons with the U.S. leading at $25.03M, and a Mexico-specific profit drill-down to identify hidden market potential. The result was a set of clear, actionable recommendations to leverage Paseo's dominance, urgently review the Enterprise segment, and expand in high-performing regions.
